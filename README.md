# heiberg
DrGeo Smalltalk Sketches for propositions of Euclid's Elements (Heiberg Teubneriana Edition)

DrGeo is a Smalltalk system for geometry (running on Cuis Smalltalk).
Greek characters can be used in DrGeo using \alpha \beta etc.
See the method #initializeNamedCharactersMap

The source of the sketches is the I.L.Heiberg and H.Menge edition of Euclid's Elementa from the Bibliotheca Scriptorum Graecorum et Romanorum Teubneriana.

Heiberg was a Danish philologist (comparative study of codex/manuscripts),
who edited many Greek mathematical texts (Euclid, Archimedes, Ptolemy, ...).

His Euclid Elements is the Classical Greek and neo-Latin edition of what could have been Euclid's "Stoicheia" ("Elementa").

This is a multi-volume critical edition of what Euclid's work could have been, mostly based on a Euclid-like manuscript from the Vatican library (I believe from Byzantine origin, tenth century ?), but also based on other manuscripts, and based on authors who commented on Euclid (that is based other mathematical texts that refer to Euclid, some from classical times such as Proclus or Simplicius).

My understanding is that the neo-Latin translation was by Heiberg and Menge themselves, and that they worked mostly from Greek language sources, but also from other medieval Latin sources (some of their neo-Latin terminology may have older precedents in Latin codices on Euclid).

My understanding is that Heiberg's Euclid Elements is thus some sort of well argumented *reconstruction* of Euclid's lost work.

The first volume of this series of books bundles book 1-4 of the geometrical Elementa's books.

Book 1 

	prop 1.  equilateral triangle (isopleuron)

Book 3

	prop 3.  bisector of tangent lines for a circle
	prop 20. central angle vs angle on perimeter (peripheria)

Book 4

	prop 4.  inscribe circle in a triangle
	prop 5.  circumscribe circle around triangle 

The second volume of the series bundles books 5-9 on proportions and arithmetic (from Euclid's geometrical point of view).

Book 6 

	prop 1.  ratio triangle / parallelogram (trigona/parallelogramma)
		 upsos (altitude)

Book 7
	prop 1.  criterion for relative prime numbers (katametre, metron, metrum)
